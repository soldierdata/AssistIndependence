Best Stair Lifts (2026)

An honest guide to choosing a stair lift: how straight and curved models differ, which features matter, and how to get quotes. Stair lifts are professionally measured and installed, so we focus on helping you compare the leading brands and find the right fit.

Last updated July 2026

Brands

Leading Stair Lift Brands

Stair lifts are not typical off-the-shelf purchases. Each unit is measured, fitted, and professionally installed for your specific staircase, so they are bought through manufacturers and their dealer networks rather than as standard retail items. Below are three of the most established brands to research when you begin comparing options.

Acorn Stairlifts

Acorn is one of the most widely known stair lift makers, best recognized for its straight-rail models. Acorn sells and installs its lifts through its own network of dealers and technicians, who handle the in-home assessment and fitting.

Bruno

Bruno is a long-established American manufacturer that produces both straight and custom curved stair lifts, along with outdoor models built for exterior stairs. Curved and outdoor configurations are made to order and installed by authorized dealers.

Harmar

Harmar is a mobility-equipment manufacturer whose product range includes straight stair lifts alongside vehicle lifts and other accessibility products. As with the other brands, its stair lifts are sold and installed through dealers.

Please note: Before choosing or installing a stair lift, consult an occupational therapist or physician. A qualified professional can assess mobility needs, evaluate whether your staircase is suitable, and confirm that a stair lift is the right solution for your situation.

Next Steps

How to Get a Quote

Because every stair lift is measured to fit an individual staircase, pricing depends on your rail length, whether the staircase is straight or curved, and any options you choose. Manufacturers and their dealers provide a free in-home assessment: a technician measures your stairs, discusses your needs, and gives you a written quote before anything is ordered.

To request an assessment or learn more, visit the manufacturers directly:

  • Acorn Stairlifts
  • Bruno
  • Harmar

Comparing quotes from more than one dealer is a good way to understand fair pricing and available options for your home.

Stair Lift Buying Guide

Straight vs. Curved Stair Lifts

Straight stair lifts work on staircases with no turns, curves, or intermediate landings. They use standard rails and cost less. Curved stair lifts require custom-manufactured rails to follow the exact contour of your staircase, significantly increasing cost but providing a solution for complex layouts.

Key Features to Consider

Look for swivel seats that rotate at the top for safer exit, folding arms and footrests to maximize stair width when not in use, and battery backup for power outages. Adjustable seat height and armrest width accommodate different body types.

Installation and Maintenance

Professional installation is required and typically takes 2-4 hours for straight rails, longer for curved. The rail mounts to the stair treads, not the wall. Annual maintenance includes lubricating the rail, checking battery health, and testing safety sensors.

Safety Features

All quality stair lifts include obstruction sensors that stop the lift if something is on the stairs, seatbelts, and swivel locks that prevent movement until the seat is locked in position. Look for pressure-sensitive edges on the footrest and carriage.
